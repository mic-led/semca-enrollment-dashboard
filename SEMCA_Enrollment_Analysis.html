Conversion Rate Signal — The Real Leverage Point
New registration conversion fell from 68.3% (Winter 2025) to 55.3% (Winter 2026) even as applications surged +41.7%. If conversion recovers to the prior-year rate, 335 projected apps × 68% = ~228 new registrations — 44% above the trend projection. Growing the winter applicant pool matters, but converting applicants is where the biggest enrollment gains are hiding.
Methodology: Three models are blended with fixed weights. Linear extrapolates the absolute annual increment (+75 apps, +18 new reg). Momentum applies the same year-over-year growth rate as 2025→2026. Moderated applies half the prior growth rate, assuming some regression toward the mean. With only 2 historical data points, all models carry high uncertainty — confidence intervals are very wide. Use the planning range as a signal for capacity and budget, not a precise target.

Key Findings

📈

Sustained Application Growth Across Every Fall Cycle

Fall applications have grown every single year: 276 (2022) → 386 (2023) → 450 (2024) → 504 (2025) — a total increase of +83% over 3 years. This reflects a strong and consistent demand pipeline. Fall 2026 is currently at 331 applications through week 9 of enrollment.

⚡

Electrical Dominates, but HVACR Is the Fastest Growing New Program

Electrical consistently represents 73–83% of all fall applications. Carpentry is second at 11–17%. HVACR launched in Fall 2022 and immediately attracted 2 applicants in its first full year — a strong debut that signals real market demand for this trade.

🏠

Sterling Heights Campus Opened With Record Enrollment

The transition from Madison Heights to the new Sterling Heights campus in Fall 2025 coincided with the highest application and registration totals in SEMCA's history. Rather than causing a disruption, the new campus appears to have energized interest and driven growth.

🔁

Returning Student Registrations Are a Growing Foundation

Returning registrations have grown from 313 (Fall 2022) to 533 (Fall 2025) — a +70.3% increase. This reflects both a growing multi-year student body and strong program retention. Returning students now make up the majority of total enrollment each year.

❄

Winter Enrollment Is Growing Strongly

Winter 2026 applications (255) outpaced Winter 2025 (180) by +41.7%. The winter cycle is becoming a meaningful second enrollment window and warrants dedicated marketing attention.

⚠

Location Data Gap Limits Campus-Level Analysis

In Fall 2024 and 2025, approximately 70–80% of applicants left the campus location field blank. This significantly limits SEMCA's ability to do precise campus-level forecasting or measure the impact of location-specific marketing. Making the field required is a quick fix with high analytical value.

To understand whether each campus is approaching its regional enrollment ceiling — how saturated the local market is relative to what's realistically reachable — three data points are needed that are not currently captured:

  • Student zip code or city of residence — allows each applicant to be assigned to a campus catchment area by drive-time radius, and enrollment to be measured per capita within that area
  • Program-level seat capacity per campus — without a known ceiling, there's no way to measure how close enrollment actually is to it; SEMCA likely tracks this internally but it isn't in JotForm
  • Regional demographic baseline — working-age population and trade-sector job demand by zip code (available from Census ACS + BLS QCEW at no cost) establishes the addressable market size each campus is drawing from

Once campus location and applicant zip code are required fields, this dashboard can calculate enrollment-per-capita by campus region, compare each campus's draw rate against its demographic footprint, and surface early signals when a campus is capturing a disproportionate share of its available market — a leading indicator of constraint well before seat limits are hit.

Recommendations

1

Front-Load Recruiting — The First 4–6 Weeks Are Critical

Week-by-week data shows the steepest application intake occurs in the first 4–6 weeks of each enrollment cycle. Concentrating social media, employer outreach, and school visit campaigns before and immediately at launch would capture a larger portion of the addressable market while intent is highest.

2

Invest in Carpentry and HVACR Program Growth

Carpentry grew from 33 applicants in 2022 to 81 in 2025. HVACR launched in 2025 with strong demand. These two programs represent SEMCA's best opportunity to diversify beyond Electrical and reduce concentration risk. Dedicated program-specific marketing (separate from the general "learn a trade" message) would accelerate this.

3

Make Campus Location and Zip Code Required Fields on Application Forms

70–80% of recent applicants skip the location field. Requiring campus selection (clear dropdown) and applicant zip code would cost SEMCA nothing to implement in JotForm and would immediately unlock campus-level enrollment forecasting, catchment-area saturation analysis, staffing planning, and marketing attribution by location — the foundation for understanding regional capacity constraints.

4

Add a Shared Identifier Across Forms to Track Conversion

Currently, applications and registrations are siloed — there is no way to link an applicant to their eventual registration. Adding a shared field (email is sufficient) would let SEMCA calculate application-to-registration conversion rate, identify where drop-off occurs, and measure the ROI of recruitment activities.

5

Run a Dedicated Winter Enrollment Campaign

Winter 2026 grew +41.7% over Winter 2025. With targeted outreach (particularly to employer partners who hire mid-year), the winter cycle could become a significantly larger second enrollment window without cannibalizing fall numbers.

6

Establish a Weekly Enrollment Pace Dashboard

This analysis can be regenerated any time the JotForm data is re-synced. Consider running the sync script weekly during enrollment periods and sharing the pace chart with leadership — it provides an early warning system if a given year is trending behind prior cycles and allows time to respond with targeted outreach.

How this works: Three models are blended. The Trend model projects last year's final forward by a damped share of the most recent year-over-year growth — stable even with little current data, and respectful of the slowing growth rate. The Velocity model OLS-fits the current year's weekly submission pattern against each prior year's normalized velocity curve. The Historical Share model divides the current total by the share of the final that prior years typically had in hand by this same week. The blend shifts from ~15% trend / 85% pace now toward 15% / 85% by week 10. Confidence reflects historical accuracy (MAPE) from a walk-forward backtest — how wrong the model has been at this exact week in prior years.
